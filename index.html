My approach is straightforward: identify a concrete problem, build a solution that works reliably, and refine it based on how it's actually used. The technology matters, but what matters more is whether the system does what it's supposed to do, day after day.

Technical Perspective

How I approach system architecture

Reliability First

Systems must work when people depend on them. SMS notifications must deliver, data must persist, interfaces must respond. This informs architecture decisions, error handling, and testing.

Structured Simplicity

Complex systems should be composed of simple, well-defined parts. Clear separation of concerns, consistent patterns, and maintainable code are more valuable than clever solutions.

Production Mindset

Systems are built for production from day one, with authentication, backups, and user management considered early.
